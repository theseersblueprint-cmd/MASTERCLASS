💬 The Holy Spirit Diagnostic — Live Dialogue

This is a real conversation. One He led me through, question by question, until I could no longer hide behind my excuses. Navigate through the dialogue as it unfolded.

H.S
Do you trust in God?
Me
What is trust?
H.S
Trust is the measure of your belief in a person, entity, or object, based on your understanding of its nature.
H.S
Do you trust in God?
Me
Partially. Because I expect that He might disappoint me. I have lived a life of disappointment. Most of what I prayed for did not come when I expected it.
H.S
If your father had unlimited resources, would you trust him completely?
Me
One hundred percent. He would always want to do what makes me happy and comfortable. Because I know his heart, and I know what he is capable of when nothing is holding him back.
H.S
So if you trust your earthly father that deeply, why not extend that same trust to God, your Heavenly Father?
H.S
So let me be clear about what you're really saying. You don't fully trust God for two reasons: you fear He will disappoint you, and you don't want to surrender control to Him. Is that correct?
Me
Both, yes. The problem is my understanding. And my human nature.
H.S
That level of self-awareness is a gift. If you can understand yourself clearly, you have found the fastest path to growth and transformation.
H.S
I designed your mind to operate on predictability. So ask yourself honestly, what does your mind associate trusting in God with?
Me
Disappointment.
H.S
Before God began anything, He had already finished it. He built the chessboard, wrote every rule that governs the game, played it from the first move to the last, and finished it — all before He built the earth.
H.S
If your father gave you a car at five years old and told you to drive, would you trust him?
Me
Honestly? I would play with the controls, crash it, get hurt, and probably carry permanent scars. A father who gives a gift that causes harm either has something wrong with his mind, or he doesn't love me.
H.S
Therefore, when God withholds something from you, it is because He does not want you to get hurt.
H.S
God's power moves upon His word. Stop measuring your prayer life by how much time you have spent praying — measure it by whether God has spoken to you, and whether you have done what He said.
Me
I now trust in the Lord. My assignment is clear — discern His will consistently. Train my discipline to obey what He tells me. Because answers are not determined by how much I pray. They are determined by whether I do what He says.

LAYER 4 OF 6

🧩 Deep Architecture — The Subconscious Mind

Your mind operates on two levels. The conscious mind makes active decisions — the part reading these words right now. And then there is your subconscious mind — the part that runs on autopilot, running approximately 95% of your daily activities.

The belief systems quietly undermining your trust in God are most likely not the ones you are consciously aware of. They are running in the background. They surface as feelings you can't fully explain, as resistance you can't name, as doubt that shows up before your conscious mind even has time to respond.

BUILDER 1

Information — What Was Uploaded Into Your Mind

+

School taught that God is a concept human beings invented to explain the origin of the universe and the mystery of life. A psychological coping mechanism. A story told by people who didn't have science yet. That seed, planted in a young mind, grows into a tree you don't notice because you grew up in its shadow.

But consciousness — the fact that matter, physical, measurable, observable matter, is aware of itself — that breaks the rules. Matter is not supposed to have consciousness. And yet here we are.

BUILDER 2

Associations — The Beliefs Others Carry Into Your Life

+

If you surround yourself with individuals who have lost hope, your subconscious begins to register hopelessness as the normal state. You don't decide to lose hope. You don't sit down and choose it. But your actions quietly begin to follow a belief system that has already given up, without you ever noticing the shift.

BUILDER 3

Environment & Culture — The Invisible Rules

+

Every week we gathered for the Sabbath. Same time, same seats, same routine. Most people were not there because they genuinely wanted to be. They were there out of fear of hell. Out of guilt. Out of social pressure. The culture produced carnal believers — people barely transformed, unable to walk in consistent victory.

Slowly, quietly, people stopped trusting God. Nobody announced it. But the subconscious conclusion spread through the congregation like a quiet agreement: it's normal for God not to answer. It's normal not to fully trust Him.

BUILDER 4

Past Experiences & Failure — The Final Break

+

I prayed. Repeatedly. Sincerely. And the answers didn't come when I expected them. I failed again and again. And my mind drew the only conclusion it knew how to draw from that data: God cannot be trusted. It became a settled belief, not a passing thought.

Church leaders placed restriction after restriction on my life. Because these people represented God to me, I concluded that God must be like that. Restrictive. Demanding. Joyless. My mind began steering me away from anything Godly — quietly, automatically.

🔓 The Four Conditions — Personal Audit

God blesses you for three reasons: to make you comfortable, to make you effective in fulfilling His will, and to be a blessing to the world. These four conditions determine whether a prayer manifests. Audit yourself honestly.

○
CONDITION 1: His Will

Am I operating within God's jurisdiction? If not, His power will only work to draw me back.

○
CONDITION 2: His Timing

Am I accepting that if the timing is not right, the answer will not manifest yet — and that is not rejection?

○
CONDITION 3: My Capacity

Do I have the spiritual and emotional maturity to carry what I'm asking for? Or would it crush me at this stage?

○
CONDITION 4: His Word

Has God given me a specific instruction? And did I obey it? Because His power moves upon His word — not my wishes.

STATUS: Mark each condition you are honestly operating in.
LAYER 6 OF 6

🪞 The Three Honest Questions

What does your mind associate trusting in God with? Disappointment? Control? Restriction? Trace that association back to its root. Name it. Write it down. Look at it plainly. Because you cannot address what you refuse to acknowledge.

Of the four mindset builders — information, associations, environment, past experience — which one shaped your distrust the most? Your answer is the specific origin point where your trust was corrupted. That is where healing begins.

When your prayers feel unanswered, do you ask: am I in His will? Have I built the capacity for what I'm asking for? Did God give me an instruction, and did I follow it? Or do you default to the old conclusion — that God cannot be trusted? That answer reveals where you are.
