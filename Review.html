The Vision

For years, I struggled deeply with Christian content. Where I came from, there was a profound lack of depth, leaving me in a constant state of confusion. But through that wrestling, I realized a fundamental truth about how human life is constructed:

Your Environment, your Associations, and your Thoughts dictate your Decisions. Your Decisions become your Actions. Your Actions solidify into your Behavior. Your Behavior constructs your Character. And your Character ultimately shapes your Destiny.

Right now, the collective destiny of many believers does not reflect the dominion Christ actually gave us.

If you look honestly at the major sectors that govern human society, the reality is sobering. Look at the top tier of leadership across these areas. What percentage of them actually confess Jesus Christ as Lord? It is a fractional minority.

  • Media and Entertainment: The major studios and conglomerates that produce the movies, music, and art our children consume are largely run by boards that do not know God. They systematically program our culture to normalize anti Christ values.
  • Government and Law: The legislators writing the policies that directly affect our families and freedoms often operate from a completely secular, humanistic worldview.
  • Economy and Business: Look at the Fortune 500. The architects controlling global wealth and resources rarely build on Kingdom principles.
  • Education: The curriculums shaping the minds of the next generation have actively removed objective, biblical truth.

And what is the church's response? Too often, believers simply fold their arms. We retreat inside the four walls of the building and just pray, expecting God to magically fix it, instead of going out into the world and becoming valuable enough to society to command change.

Because of the mockeries and compromises we have seen across churches, the world no longer takes us seriously. They look at us as people who gather because we need emotional hope, not because the God we serve is tangibly real and powerful.

Preachers are doing a wonderful job, but preaching alone will not change society.

The solution is not more preaching. The solution is raising people. If we want to change nations, we must start by transforming the individual. And to transform the individual requires exactly two words: Mindset Shift.
